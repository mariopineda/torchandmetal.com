Determine the number of shortest possibe routes to get from point A to point B. Paths must travel through indicated intermediate points.

A.

B.

C.

D.

{% if true %}

A.

4 up and 6 right, total 10 steps $\Rightarrow \displaystyle\frac{10!}{4! \times 6!} = 210$ possible paths

B.

4 up and 3 right, total 7 steps and 2 up and 4 right, total 6 $\Rightarrow \displaystyle\frac{7!}{4! \times 3!} \times \displaystyle\frac{6!}{2! \times 4!} = 525$ possible paths

C.

Same as B since one cannot go left or down. Pathways in the top left and bottom right that would prevent you to go through point C are dead ends.

D.

$\displaystyle\frac{7!}{4! \times 3!} \times 1 \times \displaystyle\frac{7!}{4! \times 3!} = 35 \times 1 \times 35 = 1225$ possible paths
