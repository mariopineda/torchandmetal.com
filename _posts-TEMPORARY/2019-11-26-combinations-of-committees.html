In a group of 12 students, there are 7 girls and 5 boys. How many 5 person committees can be made if there are...

  1. at least 3 boys on the committee?
  2. exactly 4 girls on the committe?
  3. at most 2 boys on the committee?

{% if true %}

A.

$\binom{5}{3} \times \binom{7}{2} + \binom{5}{4} \times \binom{7}{1} + \binom{5}{5} \times \binom{7}{0} = 246$ different committees.

B.

$\binom{7}{4} \times \binom{5}{1} = 175$ different committees.

C.

$\binom{7}{5} + \binom{5}{1} \times \binom{7}{4} + \binom{5}{2} \times \binom{7}{3} = 546$ different committees.
