As an athlete on a plant-based diet you need to ensure an adequate protein intake. The following are some plant-based high-protein sources.

  • Soy-based sources of protein:
    • tempeh
    • tofu
  • Legume-based sources of protein:
    • dal
    • black beans
    • hummus
    • kidney beans
    • black lentils
  • Nut/seed-bases sources of protein:
    • walnuts
    • cashews
    • chia seeds
    • hemp hearts
    • flax seeds
    • sesame seeds
    • pistachios
    • pumpkin seeds
    • sunflower seeds
  1. Your coach has instructed you that your post-workout meal has to contain 1 soy-based source of protein, 2 different legume-based proteins, and three different nut/seed-based sources of protein. How many different meals can you put together?
  2. Nuts and seeds are great as a high-protein trail mix for snacking. How many different trails mixes can you prepare that includes at least 6 different nut/seed-based sources of protein?
  3. How many different trails mixes can you prepare that includes at most 3 different nut/seed-based sources of protein?
{% if true %}

A.

The order of the different dishes does not matter. 1 soy-based source of protein and 2 different legume-based proteins and three different nut/seed-based sources of protein ('and' means multiplication). $\binom{2}{1} \times \binom{5}{2} \times \binom{9}{3} = 2 \times 10 \times 84 = 1680$ different post-workout meals.

B.

There are 9 nut/seed-based protein sources and the trail mix can consist of 6 or 7 or 8 or 9 of these ('or' means addition); $\binom{9}{6} + \binom{9}{7} + \binom{9}{8} + \binom{9}{9}= 84 + 36 + 9 + 1 = 130$ different trail mixes.

C.

The trail mix can consist of 1, 2 or 3 of these; $\binom{9}{1} + \binom{9}{2} + \binom{9}{3} = 9 + 36 + 84 = 129$ different trail mixes.
