Climate change is one of the most important environmental issues of our time. Climate change is caused by the increase in concentrations of greenhouse gases (GHGs) such as carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. These increases are primarily due to human activities such as the use of fossil fuels or agriculture. The following table summarizes the GHG emissions in Canada for different provinces between 1990 and 2017. In May 2015, Canada indicated its intent to reduce GHG emissions by 30% below 2005 levels by 2030.

The table below summarizes the GHG emissions per province for the years 1990, 2005 and 2017.

  1. Determine the measures of central tendency (mean, median) for each year. Provide all measures to the nearest tenth.
  2. Determine the measures of dispersion (range, standard deviation) for each year. Provide all measures to the nearest tenth.
  3. Interpret the descriptive statistics measures in the context of the provincial GHG emissions for the three years. What patterns do you see in the data and in the statistical measures? Does it appear that Canada is on track to reducing its GHG emissions?
