Vasa is a Swedish warship built between 1626 and 1628. The ship tipped over and sank after sailing only about 1 km into her maiden voyage. After her sinking she fell into obscurity for over 300 years until she was located again in the late 1950s in a busy shipping lane just outside Stockholm harbor. The ship was salvaged with a largely intact hull in 1961. The ship is now located in museum and is one of Sweden's most popular tourist attractions.
