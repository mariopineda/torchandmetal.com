This website is a curated repository of high school math problems aligned with the Alberta Education program of Study for grade 10-12 mathematics. The purpose of this web site is to provide practice problems for my students. This site is available to be freely used by students, teachers and members of the public as they see fit.

FAQ

Who made this web site?

I am an Edmonton-based high school math teacher.

I found an mistake in a question or solution

You can report the mistake using the "Report Errors" link on the bottom of the page where the practice problem is.

Why is a question missing a solution?

This site is permanently under construction and the development of the practice problems is an ongoing "one man show". Sometimes I fall behind a bit. Rest assure that I am always working on new practice problems and solutions as fast as I can. Check back regularly for new problems and solution.

Why are only certain courses included?

The course that are included are the ones I have been teaching since I started working on this web site. As I teach other course I will be adding these as well.

Can I use the material on this website in my own class?

Absolutelly, as long as you adhere to the copyright policy for this web site..., which is exceptionally permissive.

Why the MIT licence?

The MIT licence is an exceptionally permissive license permitting students, teachers and the general public to freely use the material from this website in whatever manner they choose to as long proper attribution is provided. The world is changing and so is education. Our education system needs to be designed for the future, not anchored in the past in archaic and restrictive copyright practices.
