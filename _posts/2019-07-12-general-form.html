Determine the equations of the lines given the following information. Express the equation in both slope y-intercept and general forms.

  1. A line that has an x-intercept at 5 and a y-intercept at 1.
  2. A line that passes through the point $f(1)=5$ and is parallel to $3x-y+5=0$.
{% capture solution %}

A.

Since at the x-intercept $y=0$ and at the y-intercept $x=0$ we have the following two points, $(5,0)$ and $(0,1)$. Using these points we can determine the slope of the line, $m=\frac{\displaystyle 0-1}{\displaystyle 5-0}=-\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}$. We can now express the equation of the line is slope y-intercept form as $y=-\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x+1$. We can convert the slope y-intercept form to general form as follows, $$ \begin{array}{rcll} y & = & -\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x+1 & \\ -\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x-y+1 & = & 0 & \mbox{Subtract y from both sides and reverse LHS and RHS} \\ (-5) \times \left(-\frac{\displaystyle 1}{\displaystyle 5}x-y+1 \right) & = & 0 \times (-5) & \mbox{Multiply both sides by -5 to eliminate negative fraction} \\ x+5y-5 & = & 0 & \mbox{Apply distributive property on the LHS} \end{array} $$

B.

Convert $3x-y+5=0$ to slope y-intercept form to determine the slope of the parallel line. Add $y$ to both sides results in $y=3x+5$ where the slope $m=3$. Since parallel lines have the same slopes $m=m_{\parallel}=3$. Since $f(1)=5 \Rightarrow$ represents the point $(1,5)$ we can now subsitute in the slope, the given point into the slope y-intercept form and solve for the y-intercept ($b$), $$ \begin{array}{rcll} y & = & mx+b \\ 5 & = & 3 \times 1 + b & \mbox{Substitue in known values} \\ 5-3 & = & b & \mbox{Add -3 to both sides} \\ 2 & = & b & \mbox{Simplify} \end{array} $$ We can now express the equation of the line in slope y-intercept form as $y=3x+2$ and in general form as $3x-y+2=0$.
