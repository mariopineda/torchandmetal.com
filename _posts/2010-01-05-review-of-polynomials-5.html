  1. A triangle has side lengths of $9a+4$, $2a+2$ and $a-4$.
    1. Determine a simplifed expression for the perimeter of this triangle.
    2. If the perimeter of this triangle is 38 cm, determine the value of $a$.
  2. A triangle has side lengths of $2b+1$, $3b-1$ and a perimeter of $13b^2+3b+2$. Determine the expression for the length of the third side.
  3. In $\triangle ABC$, $AB = AC$, $BC=b+1$ and $AB+AC+BC=3b+5$. Determine the expression for $AB$.
{% capture solution %}

1A.

\[ \begin{array}{cl} & (9a+4)+(2a+2)+(a-4) \\ = & 9a+4+2a+2+a-4 \\ = & 12a + 2 \end{array} \]

1B.

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} 12a+2 & = & 38 \\ 12a & = & 36 \\ a & = & 36 \div 12 \\ a & = & 3 \end{array} \]

2.

The length of the third side is the perimeter minus the length of the two other sides of the triangle. \[ \begin{array}{cl} & 13b^2+3b+2-(2b+1)-(3b-1) \\ = & 13b^2+3b+2-2b-1-3b+1 \\ = & 13b^2-2b+2 \end{array} \]

3.

If $x = AB = AC$, then $x+x+b+1=3b+5$. Solve for $x$. \[ \begin{array}{rcl} x+x+b+1 & = & 3b+5 \\ 2x+b+1 & = & 3b+5 \\ 2x & = & 3b+5-b-1 \\ 2x & = & 2b+4 \\ x & = & \fraction{2b+4}{2} \\ x & = & b+2 \end{array} \] Hence $AB = b+2$. {% endcapture %} {% include_relative solution.html content=solution %}
